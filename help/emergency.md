# Emergency Toolkit

Create and store encrypted emergency contacts, exit checklists, and secure deletion scripts for use in high-risk situations. All data is encrypted with your profile passphrase and never leaves your device.

---

## Who this is for

- Journalists operating in censored or high-risk regions
- Travelers who want a pre-planned response to device seizure or border crossing
- Anyone who needs a secure, offline record of critical contacts and exit steps

---

## Encrypted contacts

Store contact details — names, phone numbers, email addresses, encrypted messaging handles — in an encrypted vault. Contacts are decrypted in memory for display only and never written to disk in plaintext.

### Adding a contact

1. Click **Emergency Toolkit** in the sidebar.
2. Click **Add Contact**.
3. Enter the label and contact details.
4. Click **Save**. The entry is encrypted immediately.

### Viewing a contact

1. Click the contact in the list to expand it.
2. Details are decrypted in memory for display.

### Deleting a contact

1. Open the contact.
2. Click **Delete**.
3. Confirm when prompted. Deletion is permanent.

---

## Exit checklist

A pre-built checklist of actions to take in an emergency — device wipe steps, who to contact, what to destroy first, where to go. Customize it for your specific situation before you travel, not during.

### Using the exit checklist

1. Click **Exit Checklist** in the Emergency Toolkit.
2. Check off items as you complete them during an emergency.
3. Click **Reset Checklist** to restore all items to unchecked for the next use.

### Customizing the checklist

1. Click **Add Item** to create a new checklist entry.
2. Assign it a category — Device, Communications, Physical, or Other.
3. Drag to reorder items within a category.
4. Click **Delete** on any item to remove it permanently.

## Exporting an encrypted archive

You can export your emergency contacts and exit checklist as an encrypted archive for offline storage on a USB drive or secure location.

See [Backup & Export](backup.md) for instructions.

---

## Important notes

> **Prepare before you need it.** The emergency toolkit is most useful when set up before a high-risk situation, not during one. Build your contact list and exit checklist at home.

> **Keep your passphrase accessible.** If you are in a crisis and cannot remember your passphrase, your encrypted contacts cannot be decrypted. Consider storing your passphrase in a separate secure location such as a hardware-encrypted USB drive.

> **Deletion is permanent.** There is no undo for deleted contacts or checklist items.
